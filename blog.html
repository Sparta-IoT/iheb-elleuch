Since September 2023

Co-founding U.Stall — Software Team Lead

U.Stall started in late September 2023 when members of a one-time university project decided to build something lasting. Today we are an established student team of Mechatronics and Industrial Engineering students at Esslingen University of Applied Sciences, developing ROVs for the international MATE ROV World Championship.

I serve as Software Team Lead, responsible for software architecture, electrical integration, and communication systems. Our goal: fine-tune every detail and field an optimized ROV for the World Cup in Canada. The team operates like a company — with engineering presentations, technical documentation, marketing displays, and safety inspections alongside the pool competition itself.

Supported by mentors Thomas Hoover and Oliver Dürr, and sponsors including Andritz Schuler, VdF Esslingen, and Würth Elektronik. Follow our journey on Instagram @u.stall25.

March 1, 2025

Excursion to the German Navy

In March 2025, U.Stall traveled to Eckernförde on the Baltic Sea for an exclusive excursion with the German Navy. The visit gave our team firsthand insight into maritime technology — the same domain our ROVs are built to operate in.

Seeing professional naval systems up close reinforced why we build underwater robots: ROVs reduce risk for divers and crews while enabling inspection, exploration, and maintenance in harsh marine environments. The experience directly informed how we think about reliability, safety analysis, and real-world deployment constraints.

September 2025 – February 2026

Bachelor Thesis: LLM Chatbot for Laser Processing

At Bosch Manufacturing Solutions in Feuerbach, I completed my bachelor thesis on developing an LLM-based chatbot to support script generation for scanner-controlled laser processes — a low-resource programming environment where engineers traditionally write specialized process code by hand.

Key work included building a training dataset tailored to laser processing syntax, designing an evaluation methodology to measure programming performance of fine-tuned models, and comparing multiple LLM fine-tuning approaches on small datasets. The result: a locally deployable model that assists engineers without sending sensitive process data to external services.

This project sits at the intersection of manufacturing automation and modern AI — directly aligned with Bosch's work on laser beam shaping as a key enabler in laser material processing.

September 2024 – January 2025

Bosch Internship: Laser Beam Shaping GUI

Before my thesis, I spent five months as a Research and Software Engineering Intern in Bosch's laser technology department. I led development of a graphical user interface in C# and WPF using the MVVM pattern — giving engineers intuitive control over dynamic laser beam shaping parameters.

On the backend, I built Python routines for data acquisition, beam optics control, and process optimization, connected via gRPC interfaces to the hardware layer. The final step was validating the complete software stack on the physical prototype — bridging simulation, control code, and real laser optics.

Laser beam shaping is emerging as a critical enabler beyond simply increasing laser power: it enables finer control over material processing outcomes in additive manufacturing and precision machining.

June 15, 2024

MATE ROV World Championship 2024 — Our Debut

June 15, 2024 marked U.Stall's first appearance at the international MATE ROV Competition in Kingsport, Tennessee, USA. We entered the Pioneer class — the entry tier for newer teams — and placed 4th, a strong result for a first-year team.

The MATE ROV Competition is a global STEM challenge where students design, build, and test underwater robots to solve real-world marine problems — from offshore maintenance to environmental monitoring. Tasks range from remotely operated pool missions to autonomous segments, plus engineering presentations and technical documentation.

ROV Hans, our first robot, carried the team: laser-cut acrylic mechanics, custom control software, three cameras, and a Reuleaux triangle form factor. That foundation made the step up to Explorer class in 2025 possible.

2023 – 2026

Building the U.Stall ROV Fleet

Each competition cycle, U.Stall iterates on both the underwater vehicle and its surface float. Our fleet tells the story of continuous engineering improvement:

ROV Hans — Our first ROV, focused on core functionality. Laser-cut acrylic mechanics, custom-developed control software, three cameras, and a distinctive Reuleaux triangle shape.

ROV Luis — Designed for lower weight and smaller dimensions. Features a new gripper concept, three cameras, an optimized thruster layout, and a custom PCB for thruster control.

Float Franz — Our first surface float with a simple pipe structure, fixed cable glands, and a mounting mechanism for the ROV.

Float Clara — A stable float for Poseidon with modular construction: plastic pipe frame, syringe-based buoyancy bodies, and custom-turned end caps.
